# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

**Primary:** Men aged 18–45 experiencing anxiety, shame, or comparison pressure around body size and masculinity. They often arrive with low trust — they've encountered sensationalism, mockery, or clinical detachment elsewhere. They need to feel safe, seen, and respected within seconds.

**Secondary:** Partners, friends, and allies who want to understand or support someone dealing with these issues. NGO partners and mental health advocates seeking credible collaborators.

**Context of use:** Private browsing likely. Sensitive, often vulnerable state. Trust is established or lost in the first 5 seconds. One wrong visual signal (aggression, shame-bait, clinical coldness) ends the visit.

## Product Purpose

small p club is an awareness movement, not a measuring tape. Fashion and merch are the vehicle — the real product is self-confidence, belonging, and education.

The mission: replace shame with knowledge, replace comparison with context, replace silence with calm, sourced facts.

Success looks like: a visitor who arrived anxious leaving with a clearer, calmer perspective. A newsletter subscriber who shares a single email with someone they care about. A community where the word "size" stops being a verdict.

## Brand Personality

**Calm. Intelligent. Inclusive.**

Voice: warm editorial, not clinical. Measured, not loud. Factual, not preachy. The brand never raises its voice — it speaks like a trusted friend who happens to have done the research.

Tone: "Quiet confidence. Low ego. High intelligence." The brand educates first and sells second. It never shames to sell, never sensationalises to engage, never performs inclusion.

3-word personality: **Calm. Sourced. Human.**

## Anti-references

- **Gym-bro / Alpha-male aesthetic** — no red/black aggression, no "beast mode" energy, no dominance-as-masculinity framing. Nothing that makes men feel inadequate for not being aggressive.
- **Medical / clinical** — no sterile white-blue, no doctor's office tone, no jargon walls. This is not a health app. It must feel warm and human.
- **Sensationalist / clickbait** — no shame-trigger headlines, no "you won't believe" framing, no exploiting insecurities to drive clicks. Every claim is sourced; none are weaponised.
- **Generic corporate / SaaS** — no stock photos, no soul-free layouts that could belong to any brand. small p club has a specific visual identity and a specific voice — both must be immediately recognisable.

## Design Principles

1. **Calm before bold.** Every design decision starts from restraint. Energy and emphasis are earned, not defaulted to. The page earns the right to be loud by being quiet first.
2. **Facts as foundation, never as verdict.** Data is presented with context and compassion. Numbers are references, not rulers. The design reflects this: never reducing a human to a stat.
3. **Trust is the first product.** A user in a vulnerable state makes a trust decision in 5 seconds. Privacy, clarity, warmth, and zero manipulation are non-negotiable design requirements.
4. **One accent per screen.** The brand color system enforces focus. Turquoise means "pay attention here." Sienna means "myth." These signals lose meaning if overused.
5. **Education first, commerce second.** Every page hierarchy puts knowledge before conversion. CTAs exist, but they never interrupt; they follow.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- **WCAG AA** minimum across all surfaces
- `prefers-reduced-motion` respected on all animations (parallax, marquee, scroll reveals, count-ups) — the site has significant motion; reduced-motion alternatives are non-optional
- Semantic HTML structure for screen reader compatibility
- Colour contrast ≥4.5:1 for body text, ≥3:1 for large text
- No information conveyed by colour alone (myth/fact distinction uses chip labels, not colour only)
- Focus states visible and on-brand (dark turquoise focus ring)
- Anonymous access: no account required to access educational content; privacy-preserving by default
